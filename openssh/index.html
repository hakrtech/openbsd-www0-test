The OpenSSH project produces a FREE version of the SSH suite of network connectivity tools that increasing numbers of people on the Internet are coming to rely on.

The SSH suite includes the ssh program which replaces rlogin and telnet, and scp which replaces rcp and ftp. Many users of those other programs might not realize that their password is transmitted across the Internet unencrypted, but it is. OpenSSH encrypts all traffic (including passwords) to effectively eliminate eavesdropping attacks.

OpenSSH was developed by The OpenBSD Project, and its first release was included as a part of the OpenBSD operating system.
