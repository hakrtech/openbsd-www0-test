OpenBSD/landisk

OpenBSD/landisk runs on machines related to the IO-DATA USL-5P, using a Hitachi/Renesas SH-4 CPU.
This rather small unit (25cm x 10cm x 2cm) is sold only in Japan. This platform comes in a variety of models:

  • IO-DATA USL-5P (Japan; as shown)
  • IO-DATA HDL-U series (Japan)
  • IO-DATA UHDL-160U and UHDL-300U (May be found in USA)
  • Plextor PX-EH16L, PX-EH25L and PX-EH40L (May be found in Europe)
Note: The "Giga-landisk" machines are ARM-based (both Intel XScale & Marvell) and supported by the armish architecture.

The current port maintainers are Miod Vallat (miod@openbsd.org) and Michael Shalayeff (mickey@openbsd.org).

History:

OpenBSD/landisk is the 1st OpenBSD port to a Hitachi/Renesas SH-4 based machine. It is hoped that other SH-4 based machines will show up which are interesting enough for our user and development community, but the SH-4 processor is normally used only in true embedded products.

Current status:

Development is currently pretty active, and maybe soon it will work!
