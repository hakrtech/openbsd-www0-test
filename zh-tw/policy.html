版權採用方針

  • 目標

    著作權(版權)法是複雜的法規, 但 OpenBSD 有個簡單的方針 - OpenBSD 盡全力保有 原本 Berkeley Unix 的版權宣告.

    OpenBSD 能夠有今日的成長, 是因為 Berkeley 的 Computer Systems Research Group 所立下的榜樣, 另外由於他們與其他人的努力, 才創造出並保有較無法律問題 的原始碼散布模式.

    可以 自由地再散布 的權力. "Berkeley" Unix 的進展, 已經達 到可與許多其他作業系統發展團隊交換原始程式碼的程度了, 不管是內部或是跨團隊 的交換皆然. 瞭解有關著作權法的相關考量後, 才能交換並且再散布原始程式碼, 並且仍保有原版 權宣告的精神, 其握有散布程式碼權力的觀念, 也是促使參與其中的人們協力合作的 基石.

  • Berkeley 版權宣告

    Berkeley 版權宣告對於軟體的私人或商業使用並無任何限制, 並且僅要求一個簡單 且一致的條件: 對於任何再散布的版本中, 必須保有原本的版權宣告, 及註明原作者的 姓名列表, 純粹只有宣傳的意涵.

    以下是典型的範例: 

     * Copyright (c) 1982, 1986, 1990, 1991, 1993
 *	The Regents of the University of California.  All rights reserved.
 *
 * Redistribution and use in source and binary forms, with or without
 * modification, are permitted provided that the following conditions

 *    notice, this list of conditions and the following disclaimer.

    (注意: 在系統中的某些部份, 甚至也移除了第三, 第四點版權宣告, 讓程式碼的再散布更 為容易)

    因為 OpenBSD 的版權宣告僅止於 Berkeley 版權宣告, OpenBSD 希望可以達到與 Berkeley 同樣廣泛的散布與應用. 也因為這點, OpenBSD 不能包含有比 Berkeley 版權宣告更嚴苛的程式碼, 也不能包含更改該著作為另一種版權狀態的版權宣告, 也就 是指 OpenBSD 整體是可以自由免費的再散布的, 但是其中部份元件可能無法單獨自由 免費的再散布.

  • 著作權法

    雖然完整的著作權法已經超過本文的範圍, 一些基本的道理仍是相通的. 依據現行的 法律, 除非是有另行宣告, 否則著作權皆是隨著創作物被產生之時就隨原創者擁有的, 一般來說, 版權僅可及於基本創作本身, 而不能延伸到基於基本創作所衍生的創作物, 也不包含衍生創作物中的基本創作物.

    著作權法包函以下三種的創作物:

    原創創作物
    非基於現有創作物衍生的原創創作物
    衍生創作物
    該創作物是衍生於, 包函, 或修改現有創作物
    編輯創作物
    該創作物是編輯現有或衍生創作物而成

    著作權法的基本精神就是衍生創作物的著作權, 並不影響原基本創作作者的著作權, 只是多增加了衍生創作者的著作權而已. 同樣的, 編輯著作權並不影響其所包含創 作物的著作權, 只有新增編輯著作的權力而已.

    大家要瞭解到著作權法不管是在國內或國際上, 都受到廣泛的保障. 通常放在程式 碼中的"版權宣告"並非指著作權, 而是讓撰寫宣告的人聲明他們擁有這件作品或部 份作品的版權. 通常這些聲明伴隨著一些著作權法所註明的使用許可, 以及擁有此 著作權的所有人/散布人與這項作品的可能使用方式.

  • 使用許可 - 相對於版權的另一面

    由於著作權是來自於作品的創作, 而非經由註冊程續而來, 我們需要一個實際的 方式來擴展作品的使用許可, 這也可能包含著作權法中"正當使用"所允許的使用情況.

    這項許可通常以發行版本或使用許可的方式附於作品中, 其中說明了著作權法規範 以外的允許使用情況. 其中一項極端的例子是 public domain, 意即創作者聲明 他對於該作品的使用方式並不設定限制, 限制較多的聲明可能只限於著作權法中允許 的使用方式, 或是較偏狹的聲明, 讓作品難以使用的各種限制條件.

    同樣的, 發行版本或使用許可的方式只限於該聲明者本身的創作部份, 基於前述創作而 生的衍生創作, 則無法對原創作物的許可權做出或增或減的權力聲明.

    由於著作權是來自於作品的創作, 而非經由註冊程續而來, 移除或修改版權宣告 或是隨作品發行的聲明並不會對著作權有任何影響, 只會對修改者基於該作 品所衍生的新作品有效用而已. 相同的, 增加聲明與使用條件時, 假如與原有聲明與使用條件相衝突, 也無法產生 效用, 做出新增聲明與使用條件者的行為, 只會讓人質疑她的修改作品或衍生作品 是否真的可以合法的拿來使用.

    最後一點, 發行版本通常伴隨著該創作物推出. 這意指如果原創作人發行作品時 也附上某些使用許可條件, 則這些使用許可是公平的適用於所有合法取得該作品 的所有人. 也就是說已經發出的使用許可, 原創作人並無法溯及既往的收回他人 的使用權力. 相同的, 假如該原創者決定將作品商品化, 雖然他可以對基於該作 品的衍生創作做出更嚴苛的使用條件, 但他並無法收回或修改已賣出的原創作使 用權.

  • 特殊案例

    這個部份是 OpenBSD 對於一些常見版權宣告的使用狀況摘要.

    Berkeley

    The Berkeley copyright is the model for the OpenBSD copyright. It retains the rights of the copyright holder, while imposing minimal conditions on the use of the copyrighted material. Material with Berkeley copyrights, or copyrights closely adhering to the Berkeley model can generally be included in OpenBSD.

    AT&T

    While AT&T holds the copyrights to much "Unix" code and documentation, OpenBSD is based largely on Berkeley (BSD) distributions that contain only material known to be free of AT&T copyrights, or material to which AT&T has abandoned its copyright or included licensing terms similar to the Berkeley terms. No material subject to restrictive AT&T copyrights can be included in OpenBSD.

    Examples of AT&T code, included subject to non-restrictive copyrights, include some system sources such as init_main.c and the AT&T version of awk.

    DEC, Sun, other manufacturers/software houses.

    In general OpenBSD does not include material copyrighted by manufacturers or software houses. Material may be included where the copyright owner has granted general permission for reuse without conditions, with terms similar to the Berkeley copyright, or where the material is the product of an employee and the employer's copyright notice effectively releases any rights they might have to the work.

    Carnegie-Mellon (CMU, Mach)

    The Carnegie-Mellon copyright is similar to the Berkeley copyright, except that it requests that derivative works be made available to Carnegie-Mellon. Because this is only a request and not a condition, such material can still be included in OpenBSD. It should be noted that existing versions of Mach are still subject to AT&T copyrights, which prevents the general distribution of Mach sources.

    Apache

    The Apache copyright is similar to the Berkeley copyright, except that it stipulates that products derived from the code may not have "Apache" in their name. The purpose of this clause is to avoid a situation in which another party releases a modified version of the code named in such a way to make users think that it is the "official" version. This is not an issue with OpenBSD because OpenBSD is a Compilation, and not a Derived Work.

    GNU General Public License, GPL, LGPL, copyleft, etc.

    The GNU Public License and licenses modeled on it impose the restriction that source code must be distributed or made available for all works that are derivatives of the GNU copyrighted code.

    While this may be a noble strategy in terms of software sharing, it is a condition that is typically unacceptable for commercial use of software. As a consequence, software bound by the GPL terms can not be included in the kernel or "runtime" of OpenBSD, though software subject to GPL terms may be included as development tools or as part of the system that are "optional" as long as such use does not result in OpenBSD as a whole becoming subject to the GPL terms.

    As an example, some ports include GNU Floating Point Emulation - this is optional and the system can be built without it or with an alternative emulation package. Another example is the use of GCC and other GNU tools in the OpenBSD tool chain - it is quite possible to distribute a system for many applications without a tool chain, or the distributor can choose to include a tool chain as an optional bundle which conforms to the GPL terms.

    NetBSD

    Much of OpenBSD is originally based on and evolved from NetBSD, since some of the OpenBSD developers were involved in the NetBSD project. The general NetBSD license terms are compatible with the Berkeley license and permit such use. Material subject only to the general NetBSD license can generally be included in OpenBSD.

    In the past, NetBSD has included material copyrighted by individuals who have imposed license conditions beyond that of the general NetBSD license, but granted the NetBSD Foundation license to distribute the material. Such material can not be included in OpenBSD as long as the conditions imposed are at odds with the OpenBSD license terms or releases from those terms are offered on a discriminatory basis.

    FreeBSD

    Most of FreeBSD is also based on Berkeley licensed material or includes copyright notices based on the Berkeley model. Such material can be included in OpenBSD, while those parts that are subject to GPL or various individual copyright terms that are at odds with the OpenBSD license can not be included in OpenBSD.

    Linux

    Most of Linux is subject to GPL style licensing terms and therefore can not be included in OpenBSD. Individual components may be eligible, subject to the terms of the originator's copyright notices. Note that Linux "distributions" may also be subject to additional copyright claims of the distributing organization, either as a compilation or on material included that is not part of the Linux core.

    X, XFree86

    X and XFree86 are not parts of OpenBSD, rather X or XFree86 is distributed with many OpenBSD ports as a convenience to the user, subject to applicable license terms.

    Shareware, Charityware, Freeware, etc.

    Most "shareware" copyright notices impose conditions for redistribution, use or visibility that are at conflict with the OpenBSD project goals. Review on a case-by-case basis is required as to whether the wording of the conditions is acceptable in terms of conditions being requested vs. demanded and whether the spirit of the conditions is compatible with goals of the OpenBSD project.

    Public Domain

    While material that is truly entered into the "Public Domain" can be included in OpenBSD, review is required on a case by case basis. Frequently the "public domain" assertion is made by someone who does not really hold all rights under Copyright law to grant that status or there are a variety of conditions imposed on use. For a work to be truly in the "Public Domain" all rights are abandoned and the material is offered without restrictions.
